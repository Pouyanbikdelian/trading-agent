The Trading Agent

An automated, multi-agent trading system for Interactive Brokers — a committee of AI analysts that debates daily, a simulated portfolio manager that trades weekly, and mechanical guards that protect capital around the clock.

1What it is

Two systems share one brain. The paper trader — a rules-based momentum strategy (Top-8, S&P 500) — places real orders in an IBKR paper account every Friday. Alongside it, an agent layer reads markets, news and macro data, debates, and runs its own fully simulated $1M portfolio. The agents never touch the real order path: a hard-blocking risk manager is the only gateway to orders, and every agent output is advisory, journaled, and graded. After an observation period, the agent PM either earns real (paper) capital on evidence — or doesn't.

2How a trading day flows

All times UTC, weekdays. Quiet markets cost nothing — the LLM only runs when scheduled or when a tripwire fires.

3The committee — eight voices, one verdict

Each weekday (and on demand via /committee), seven specialists read different slices of the data — deliberately, to prevent echo chambers — and publish a stance plus a falsifiable prediction (ticker, direction, horizon, confidence).

The specialists

  • Quant — trusts numbers: momentum, vol surface, macro dial
  • Narrator — geopolitics, central banks, crowd psychology
  • Street — sell-side consensus vs. price action
  • Position Coach — our book only: entries, P&L, thesis drift
  • Risk Officer — what can hurt us this week
  • Trader — the tape, liquidity, this-week timing
  • Scout — hunts the next big sector from headlines + momentum; committed capital outranks commentary

Then the adversaries

The Challenger attacks the committee's consensus — crowding, overconfidence, what everyone is ignoring — and states what evidence would prove each take wrong.

The Manager weighs every voice by its graded track record (not eloquence) and rules: risk-on, neutral, or risk-off — with the one thing that would change its mind.

Digest → Telegram. Full transcript → /detail. Every prediction → the scorecard.

4The agent PM — a $1M sleeve on probation

Every Monday the PM reads the week's rulings, each agent's calibration, its own performance vs SPY, and fresh market context — then sets target weights over a whitelist of 21 liquid ETFs plus every S&P 500 / Nasdaq-100 / Russell 1000 stock. Hard caps it cannot argue with: 25% per ETF, 10% per stock, 40% per correlated cluster (semis + mega-tech count as one), long-only, no leverage. Hallucinated tickers are silently dropped to cash. Fills are simulated at the close with 10 bps costs; equity is marked daily against SPY. The dashboard's Strategy Race chart is its public trial.

5Protection — three independent layers

① Sentinel (intraday tripwires)

Free mechanical checks every 15 min: SPY ≤ −1.5%, VIX +20%, any holding −5%. Only a tripped wire wakes the AI to judge: noise or systemic? Real trouble → Telegram alert + auto-convened committee, minutes after the shock.

② Trailing stops (auto-exit)

Each position's stop follows its peak at a distance sized to that stock's own volatility (3× daily move, floor 8%, cap 20% — squeeze-resistant). Never sells strength; exits flow through the same audited pipeline as a manual /close. /hold exempts any position.

③ Profit ratchet (portfolio)

Arms once equity is up 20%; warns loudly after giving back 40% of the peak gain. "Secure what you made" — the decision stays human.

How a trailing stop rides a winner

The stop only rises. A +140% runner is never sold on the way up — only on a real breakdown from its peak, keeping most of the gain.

The learning loop

Every prediction is graded nightly against real prices → per-agent calibration (hit rate, Brier score) → the Manager trusts proven voices more. Each Friday the Historian distills ≤2 candidate lessons from the week ("gold breaking down while equities hold = risk-on rotation"); a lesson must survive 3 weeks of supporting evidence before agents may treat it as truth. The system gets harder to fool every week.

6Safety principles (non-negotiable)

  • One gate to orders. Strategies and agents emit signals; only the hard-blocking risk manager creates orders. Nothing bypasses it.
  • Paper until proven. Live trading requires two explicit flags (TRADING_ENV=live + ALLOW_LIVE_TRADING=true) and is never enabled by automation.
  • Agents advise, humans decide. Defensive moves (/mode defense, /flatten, /halt) are operator commands; the system makes them timely, not automatic.
  • Everything is journaled. Takes, objections, rulings, trades, lessons — auditable, graded, and visible on the dashboard.

Reading the dashboard

  • Portfolio — equity, Strategy Race (paper vs agent PM vs SPY, rebased to 100), posture history, sector momentum, headlines, schedule, system health.
  • Macro — yield curve, VIX structure, breadth, credit; each card carries a plain-language interpretation, color-coded.
  • Economy — inflation vs policy rate, US/ECB/Japan rates, housing, labor, credit & liquidity, consumer.
  • Memory — agent calibration, source trust, the lesson book.

The road to "pro"

Now → mid-July: observe. Judge the agent PM on the Strategy Race: alpha vs SPY, max drawdown, rationale quality.
If it earns it: wire the PM to emit signals through the real risk manager as a small (~20%) paper sleeve.
Only after ≥30 clean paper days: discuss live, sized down, double-locked.
