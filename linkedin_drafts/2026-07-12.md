## POST

This week my trading system sold a position I would have kept.

I sat there with my coffee reading its reasoning. Eight AI agents had debated it overnight. The risk voice won the argument. My gut said they were wrong.

I didn't override it. Not because I trust machines more than myself, but because I've spent months watching my gut lose to the test results. Every time I backtested one of my "obvious" calls, most of them were noise I'd mistaken for insight.

That's what nobody told me about building an automated trader. The hard part was never the code. It was accepting the evidence about my own judgment.

The system paper-trades through Interactive Brokers. No real money yet. It has to earn that, same as I had to learn to get out of its way.

Have you ever built something that ended up correcting you?
